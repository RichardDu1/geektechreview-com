## Overview

This workflow enables emergency managers to rapidly assess damage after natural disasters using AI-assisted drone mapping. By combining drone imagery with AI analysis, you can generate actionable damage maps within hours instead of days.

## Prerequisites

- DJI Mavic 3 Enterprise or similar drone with RTK module
- Pix4Dreact license (free for first 30 days)
- DroneDeploy account (free tier available)
- Picterra account for AI damage detection
- Laptop with internet connection

## Step 1: Pre-Flight Planning

1. **Identify target area**: Use satellite imagery or prior maps to define the disaster zone.
2. **Check airspace**: Use FAA B4UFLY or local authority app to ensure no flight restrictions.
3. **Set flight parameters**: In DJI Pilot app, set altitude (100-120m), overlap (75% front, 60% side), and grid pattern.

## Step 2: Conduct Aerial Survey

1. **Launch drone**: Ensure GPS lock and RTK correction (if available).
2. **Execute autonomous flight**: Use DJI Pilot's mapping mode to capture images.
3. **Monitor battery**: Swap batteries as needed; typical coverage is ~1 sq km per battery.

## Step 3: Process Imagery with Pix4Dreact

1. **Import images**: Transfer SD card to laptop, open Pix4Dreact.
2. **Create project**: Select images, set coordinate system (WGS84).
3. **Generate orthomosaic**: Click "Process" – takes 30-60 minutes for 500 images.
4. **Export**: Save as GeoTIFF and share with team via cloud.

## Step 4: AI Damage Detection with Picterra

1. **Upload orthomosaic**: Log into Picterra, create new detector for "Building Damage".
2. **Train or use pre-trained model**: Use existing model for structural damage.
3. **Run detection**: AI identifies collapsed buildings, debris, and flooded areas.
4. **Export results**: Download GeoJSON or shapefile with damage polygons.

## Step 5: Visualize in DroneDeploy

1. **Import orthomosaic**: Drag GeoTIFF into DroneDeploy map.
2. **Overlay damage layer**: Upload Picterra GeoJSON as a layer.
3. **Add annotations**: Mark critical infrastructure (hospitals, roads).
4. **Share map**: Generate a public link for field teams and command center.

## Step 6: Generate Report

1. **Use DroneDeploy's report tool**: Create PDF with before/after comparison.
2. **Include statistics**: Number of damaged buildings, area affected.
3. **Distribute**: Email to stakeholders and upload to incident management system.

## Tips for Success

- Always have backup batteries and memory cards.
- Use RTK for accurate georeferencing (critical for overlaying with GIS).
- Process imagery in parallel on multiple laptops for large areas.
- Test AI model on similar disaster data before deployment.

## Comparison with Alternatives

- **Manual ground survey**: Takes days, dangerous, less accurate.
- **Satellite imagery**: Lower resolution, delayed by hours.
- **Manned aircraft**: Expensive, slower to deploy.

This workflow combines the speed of drones with the intelligence of AI, giving emergency managers a decisive advantage in the critical first 72 hours.
