## Step 1: Capture Room Geometry and Seating Layout

Use a laser distance measurer or a 3D scanning app (e.g., RoomScan Pro) to create a detailed floor plan. Note:
- Room dimensions (length, width, height)
- Wall materials (drywall, concrete, glass)
- Seating positions (distance from walls, height of ear level)
- Existing furniture and obstructions

Export the plan as a DXF or JSON file.

## Step 2: Input Data into AI Analysis Tool

Open **Room Acoustics AI** (roomacousticsai.com) and upload your floor plan. The tool will:
- Calculate reverberation time (RT60) based on materials
- Identify reflection points and standing wave modes
- Suggest optimal speaker positions for 5.1, 7.1, or Atmos setups

Adjust parameters like speaker type (bookshelf, floor-standing) and desired soundstage width.

## Step 3: Generate Placement Recommendations

Run the analysis. The AI outputs:
- X/Y coordinates for each speaker (front L/R, center, surround, height channels)
- Recommended toe-in angles and elevation
- Subwoofer placement for even bass response

Export the report as a PDF or import into **Speaker Calc Pro** (speakercalcpro.com) for 3D visualization.

## Step 4: Validate with Simulation

In Speaker Calc Pro, load the AI recommendations and simulate sound propagation. Check:
- Coverage at each seating position
- Frequency response uniformity
- Avoidance of nulls and peaks

Adjust placements manually if needed, then lock the final positions.

## Step 5: Generate Installer Checklist

Use **Sound Placement GPT** (custom GPT in ChatGPT) to create a step-by-step checklist:
- Mounting heights and angles
- Wire routing paths
- Calibration sequence

Include notes for acoustic treatments (bass traps, diffusers) based on AI suggestions.

## Step 6: On-Site Implementation

Follow the checklist. Use laser levels and angle finders to ensure precision. After installation, run a calibration mic (e.g., Audyssey, Dirac) to fine-tune.

## Step 7: Client Handoff

Provide the client with:
- Final placement diagram
- Explanation of why each speaker is placed where
- Maintenance tips (e.g., avoid moving furniture that affects acoustics)
