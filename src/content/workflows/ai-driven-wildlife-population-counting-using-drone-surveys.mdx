## Overview

This workflow enables wildlife biologists to count animal populations using drone imagery and AI-powered object detection. By automating image analysis, you can cover large areas quickly and reduce human error.

## Prerequisites

- **Drone**: DJI Mavic 3 Enterprise (or similar with RGB camera and RTK GPS)
- **Software**: DroneDeploy (flight planning), Picterra (AI detection), Google Earth Engine (optional for large-scale analysis)
- **Skills**: Basic drone piloting, familiarity with GIS concepts

## Step 1: Plan Your Survey

1. **Define Area**: Use Google Earth Engine or QGIS to delineate survey boundaries.
2. **Set Flight Parameters**: In DroneDeploy, create a mapping mission with 80% front overlap and 70% side overlap. Altitude: 80-120m depending on target species size.
3. **Schedule Flight**: Choose time of day with optimal lighting (early morning/late afternoon) and minimal animal movement.

## Step 2: Conduct Drone Flight

1. **Pre-flight Check**: Ensure battery full, SD card inserted, GPS lock.
2. **Launch**: Use DroneDeploy app to start autonomous mission. Monitor battery and weather.
3. **Post-flight**: Download images to computer or cloud.

## Step 3: Process Images with AI

1. **Upload to Picterra**: Create a new project and upload orthomosaic or individual images.
2. **Train Detector**: If needed, annotate a few animals (e.g., 50-100) to train a custom model. Alternatively, use pre-trained models for common species.
3. **Run Detection**: Set confidence threshold (e.g., 0.7) and run inference. Review results and export as GeoJSON.

## Step 4: Analyze and Validate

1. **Import to GIS**: Load GeoJSON into QGIS or Google Earth Engine.
2. **Manual Validation**: Randomly check 10% of detections to estimate accuracy.
3. **Calculate Population**: Use mark-recapture or distance sampling methods if applicable. For simple counts, sum detections.

## Step 5: Report and Archive

1. **Generate Report**: Include map, count, confidence intervals, and flight metadata.
2. **Archive Data**: Store raw images, detection results, and flight logs for future reference.

## Tips

- Use RTK GPS for accurate geolocation.
- Avoid flights during rain or high wind.
- For dense vegetation, consider thermal cameras.

## Troubleshooting

- **False positives**: Adjust confidence threshold or retrain model with more examples.
- **Missed animals**: Lower flight altitude or increase overlap.
